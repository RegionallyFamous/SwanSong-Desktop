## SwanSong 0.4.1 beta — Follow the Source

SwanSong 0.4.1 makes Translation Lab better at answering a practical reverse-
engineering question: which private cartridge data actually produced the
visible thing you selected? ABI 9 adds sprite-attribute ownership, strengthens
the private evidence contract, and creates a guarded handoff to static-analysis
tools without treating their output as patch authorization.

### Trace sprite attributes, not just backgrounds

- **Select sprite ownership directly.** Upstream source probes can now isolate
  `spriteAttribute` alongside map, raster, and palette components.
- **Keep the winning sprite's evidence.** Private project artifacts retain the
  selected sprite's OAM address and byte count plus its final CPU writer.
- **Stay honest about uncertain dataflow.** Conservative traces retain their
  first reason and V30 origin privately and remain incomplete instead of being
  promoted as exact lineage.
- **Keep sensitive details local.** MCP receives only geometry, counts, hashes,
  and completeness flags. OAM addresses, CPU writers, cartridge ranges,
  executed-read context, mapper values, and conservative origins never leave
  the Translation Lab project through SwanSong's tool responses.

### Hand verified evidence to an analyzer

A current, complete ABI 9 source probe can now produce a deterministic private
seed for Ghidra or pypcode. The seed carries exact payload ranges and the
executed caller, operand, mapper, and sprite-attribute anchors needed to begin
bounded static analysis from a SwanSong-authenticated runtime fact.

The export is deliberately guarded. It revalidates the project binding,
canonical artifact, exact lineage, address arithmetic, mapper resolution, and
consumer scope before writing anything. It returns only source-free counts and
hashes to automation, and it never authorizes a ROM patch by itself.

### Larger evidence, stricter boundaries

- The shared private source-evidence limit is now 64 MiB.
- A selected probe may normalize up to 256 disjoint source ranges.
- Genuine per-byte overflow, unknown lineage, ambiguous ancestry, or
  conservative lineage still fails closed.
- The private source-probe and source-free report schemas advance to v4. The
  Evidence browser continues to read v1-v3 artifacts; static seed export
  requires a complete current v4 artifact.
- Static-analysis validation accepts the engine's full-width 16-bit mapper
  state when its fixed-window cartridge resolution is exact.

### Before you install

SwanSong 0.4.1 requires macOS 14 or later and supports Apple silicon and Intel
Macs. No games or original firmware are included. Add only game, homebrew, and
project material you own or are authorized to use.

This beta must be selected explicitly from GitHub Releases. Stable-only clients
remain on their selected channel; beta-enabled clients can discover 0.4.1 after
its signed, notarized prerelease and beta appcast entry are published. Verify
the universal app ZIP against `SHA256SUMS.txt` and the matching release manifest
before a manual install.

Restart SwanSong and the MCP client after updating so the installed ABI 9
engine and fifteen-tool automation surface are both reloaded.

### Best beta targets

- Reach a screen containing a sprite, probe one small native rectangle, select
  only sprite attributes, and confirm the private owner and source evidence is
  component-scoped.
- Export a seed from one complete exact probe and confirm a conservative,
  unknown, stale, or incomplete probe refuses export.
- Open older v1-v3 evidence in the Evidence browser and confirm it remains
  readable without being treated as current ABI 9 promotion evidence.
- Re-run paired capture and ordinary map, raster, and palette probes to confirm
  the ABI 8 workflows remain deterministic.

### Known limits

- A static-analysis seed is a bounded starting point, not a decompilation, a
  pointer-table claim, or permission to patch a cartridge component.
- Source provenance intentionally stops when ancestry, consumer scope, replay,
  or runtime ownership cannot be proved.
- SwanSong Studio remains a developer preview and still requires a separately
  selected SwanSong SDK 0.2.0-or-newer checkout plus its external toolchain.
- Commercial-title compatibility is still being validated. **Reached Video**
  is not an end-to-end verdict or original-hardware accuracy claim.
- The Homebrew Catalog and SwanSong Core download paths remain unavailable
  until their independent signed publication gates pass.
