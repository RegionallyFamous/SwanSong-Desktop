Follow the source.

SwanSong 0.4.1 gives Translation Lab an ABI 9 path from a visible sprite to its private OAM ownership and cartridge lineage, plus a guarded handoff to static-analysis tools.

  • Trace sprites directly. Select sprite attributes independently from map, raster, and palette components.
  • Keep provenance private. OAM addresses, CPU writers, cartridge ranges, mapper context, and conservative origins stay inside the project.
  • Start analysis from a fact. Export a deterministic private Ghidra or pypcode seed from a current, complete source probe.
  • Handle real evidence safely. Source artifacts may grow to 64 MiB and 256 normalized ranges while uncertain lineage still fails closed.
  • Preserve prior work. The Evidence browser continues to read v1-v3 source artifacts after the move to ABI 9 schema v4.

Restart SwanSong and the MCP client after updating. Static-analysis seeds do not authorize patches, and private project evidence never leaves through SwanSong's tool responses. Read the full release notes.
